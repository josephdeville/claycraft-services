**What TO Sell:**

- Transformation through velocity (ship campaigns weekly vs. quarterly)
- Methodology transfer (framework for their own discovery)

**Engagement Structure:**
- 30-day transformation sprints
- Clear entrance/exit criteria
- Step-wise change (not incremental improvement)
- Maximum 7 clients at a time
- Maximum 3 months per engagement
- Exit when you care more than they do

## Client Acquisition Challenge

### The Core Problem
**Effort vs. Results Mismatch:**
- **Outreach Volume:** Thousands of messages sent to RevOps managers and GTM VPs
- **Response Rate:** Zero positive responses
- **Diagnosis:** Messaging problem, not effort problem

**Why Previous Approaches Failed:**
- Too generic and consultant-heavy
- Asked discovery questions instead of demonstrating expertise
- No specific technical problem identification
- Positioned as commodity service provider
- Failed to show understanding of prospects' exact situations

### Current Prospecting Strategy Shift

**Philosophy Change:**
- FROM: Generic outreach asking broad questions
- TO: Pain-hunting with specific technical problem identification
- FROM: "Can I help you with RevOps?"
- TO: "Here's the specific technical problem you have [with evidence]"

**New Tactical Approaches:**

1. **Failed Hire Hunt**
   - Identify companies with failed GTM/RevOps hiring attempts
   - Evidence: Stale job postings, reposted roles, abandoned searches
   - Hypothesis: Failed hire = validated pain + budget + urgency

2. **Agency Refugee Approach**
   - Find companies burned by offshore lead gen agencies
   - Evidence: Negative G2 reviews mentioning specific vendors
   - Hypothesis: Bad agency experience = distrust of promises, openness to methodology

3. **Tool Buyer Who Can't Ship**
   - Identify companies with Clay subscriptions but low campaign velocity
   - Evidence: Clay workspace analysis, LinkedIn hiring signals
   - Hypothesis: Bought tools but lack expertise to execute

4. **LinkedIn Frustration Mining**
   - Scrape engagement on GTM pain-point posts
   - Evidence: Specific complaints about attribution, CRM chaos, alignment
   - Hypothesis: Public complaint = active pain + potential urgency

### Evidence-Based Outreach Requirements
Every prospect interaction must include:
- Specific technical problem identification (not generic questions)
- Publicly available evidence supporting the diagnosis
- Connection to concrete business impact
- Demonstration of understanding their exact situation

**Example Evolution:**
- ❌ "Are you struggling with attribution?"
- ✅ "Your Google Ads spend jumped 40% in Q3 but your closed-won deals tagged to paid declined 15%. That's either broken attribution or a channel efficiency problem - both worth $X in wasted spend."

## Key Insights & Learnings

### What Doesn't Work
- Generic RevOps consultant positioning
- Asking discovery questions without demonstrating expertise
- Promising outcomes (leads, meetings) you can't control
- Competing on deliverables with commodity providers
- Creating client dependency on your lead flow

### What Should Work (Being Validated)
- GTM Engineer technical identity
- Evidence-based prospecting with specific problem identification
- Pain-hunting methodology using public data synthesis
- Selling velocity and transformation over deliverables
- Making clients independent and dangerous

### Operating Principles
1. Show don't tell - prove expertise through permissionless value
2. Specific beats generic - evidence of their problem > discovery questions
3. Velocity beats perfection - rapid iteration > perfect targeting
4. Independence beats dependency - build systems, not reliance
5. Methodology beats promises - sell capability, not certainty

### Critical Differentiators
- Not selling lead generation (competing with offshore agencies)
- Not selling outcomes (competing with broken promises)
- Selling rapid experimentation frameworks (competing with slow internal execution)
- Selling transformation sprints (competing with endless consulting engagements)
